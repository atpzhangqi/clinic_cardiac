The atrial branch which gives off the SA node/nodal artery (in ~ 60 % of hearts), which runs along the anterior RA to the SVC, encircling it
before reaching the SA node. This is the second branch of the RCA (the first one is the conus artery) and in 38% of cases from the LCX and
from both arteries in 3%.(Kyriakidis MK, Kourouklis CB, Papaioannou JT, Christakos SG, Spanos GP, Avgoustakis DG. Sinus node
coronary arteries studied with angiography. Am J Cardiol. 1983 Mar 1;51:749-750.). The sinoatrial nodal artery is an artery of the heart
which supplies the SA-Node, the natural pacemaker of the heart, and arises from the RCA in around 60% of cases. In about 40% of cases, the
sinoatrial artery is a branch of the LCX.(Pejković B, Krajnc I, Anderhuber F, Kosutić D (July 2008). "Anatomical aspects of the arterial
blood supply to the sinoatrial and atrioventricular nodes of the human heart". Journal of International Medical Research. 36 (4): 691–
8. doi:10.1177/147323000803600410. PMID 18652764.) In less than 1% of cases, the artery has an anomalous origin directly from the
