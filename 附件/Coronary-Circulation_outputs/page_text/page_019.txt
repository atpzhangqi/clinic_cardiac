3.
Atrioventricular nodal branch: The atrioventricular nodal branch is a cardiac artery that is crucial because it feeds the AV-Node. In over 80%
of hearts it arises as a distal branch from the RCA near the crux of the heart. In 18% the AV-Node instead receives blood from the LCX. In
2% of cases, the vascular supply to the AV-Node arises from both the RCA and the LCX (Sow ML, Ndoye JM, Lo EA. The artery of the
atrioventricular node: an anatomic study based on 38 injection-dissections. Surg Radiol Anat 1996;18:183–187)
